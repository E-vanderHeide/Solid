Visualizing the Visualization Studio

by Pruek Laochaiyapruek

Design brief

The charts presented on this website are exhibiting the Facebook page activity of the Visualization Studio at KTH. The first chart presents all posts from the page using the treemap visualisation technique. The second chart summarises the page activity using the parallel coordinates.

The aim of these charts is to be provide ambient displays. Therefore, the treemaps focuses on only one topic which is the posts from the page. All posts are categorised into five categories with different colours and sectioned by the number of likes or the number of posts. Audiences can zoom-in, zoom-out, or click on the text to go to the post on the page.

On the other hand, the parallel coordinates display the overall page statistics. Audiences can move an axis to restructure the graph, or brush on an axis to focus on a certain part of the data-set.

As a peripheral display, dark colour scheme is applied in order to reduce the brightness of the screen and to avoid distracting users. Hence, the light coloured elements are used to provide needed information and make contrast. San serif fonts are used to support readability. For narrow or small space, condensed font is applied.

Reference

Pousman, Z., & Stasko, J. (2006, May). A taxonomy of ambient information systems: four patterns of design. In Proceedings of the working conference on Advanced visual interfaces (pp. 67-74). ACM.
